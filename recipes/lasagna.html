Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that's beloved around the world for its rich layers and comforting flavors. It's made by stacking wide, flat pasta sheets with a variety of fillings, typically including a savory mixture of ground meat (like beef or sausage), rich tomato sauce, and creamy b√©chamel or ricotta cheese. Often, the dish is also layered with grated mozzarella and Parmesan, which melt and brown beautifully during baking, creating a golden, bubbly top.

Lasagna is typically baked in a large dish, allowing the flavors to meld together as the pasta absorbs the sauces, resulting in a hearty, satisfying meal. The beauty of lasagna lies in its versatility; it can be customized with various ingredients like spinach, mushrooms, or even eggplant for a vegetarian twist. Served hot and often paired with a side of garlic bread or a fresh salad, lasagna is a crowd-pleaser that works equally well for casual family dinners or festive gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • Meat: This super meaty lasagna has sweet Italian sausage and lean ground beef.
  • Onion and garlic: An onion and two cloves of garlic are cooked with the meat to add tons of flavor.
  • Tomato products: You'll need a can of crushed tomatoes, two cans of tomato sauce, and two cans of tomato paste.
  • Sugar: Two tablespoons of white sugar add subtle sweetness and enhance the flavor of the sauce.
  • Spices and seasonings: This lasagna recipe is flavored with fresh parsley, dried basil leaves, salt, Italian seasoning, fennel seeds, and black pepper.
  • Lasagna noodles: Use store-bought or homemade lasagna noodles.
  • Cheeses: Parmesan, mozzarella, and ricotta cheese make this lasagna extra decadent.
  • Egg: An egg helps bind the ricotta so it doesn't ooze out of the lasagna when you cut into it.

Steps:

The detailed layering instructions can be found in the recipe below, but this is the order you'll follow:

  1. Meat sauce
  2. Noodles
  3. Ricotta mixture
  4. Mozzarella slices
  5. Meat sauce
  6. Parmesan cheese
  7. Repeat the layers, then top with the remaining Parmesan.
